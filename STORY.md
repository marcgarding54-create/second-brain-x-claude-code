# My Story — Why I Built This

My name is Marc. Four months ago, I was using AI the same way most people do.

Ask a question. Get an answer. Move on.

No real idea of what was actually possible. No sense of how fast this technology was moving, or what it could mean for the way we live and work.

---

## No Tech Background. At All.

I want to be clear about this: I have no IT education, no coding skills, no system administration background. None.

What I do have is this:

- I know how to gather information and find the missing pieces
- I know how to define a goal, break it into steps, and work through them systematically
- I can visualize what a result should look like — and stay focused until I get there
- I reflect honestly: what worked, what didn't, and how to improve next time
- I lead teams, develop people, build structures, and think in processes

I do this professionally. I'm a Team Leader at Amazon — responsible for planning, capacity management, and getting a team to deliver results, day after day.

---

## The Insight That Changed Everything

At some point I realized: **this is exactly what I do with people — just now with AI.**

Give clear direction. Define the goal. Break down the task. Review the result. Improve the process. Build something that works without you having to babysit it.

That mental model — team leader thinking, not coder thinking — turned out to be exactly the right way to work with AI agents.

I didn't need to learn to code. I needed to learn to brief, to orchestrate, to iterate. That I already knew.

---

## What Four Months Built

Starting from zero, learning through conversations with AI, through GitHub repositories shared by generous developers, through YouTube creators who put in hours so others don't have to — I built this:

A complete, reproducible framework that combines a personal Second Brain (Obsidian + AI assistant), a development workspace (Claude Code + Skills + Agents), and a 24/7 automation layer (n8n). All connected. All running locally on your machine.

I want to be honest: I couldn't have done this without the people who shared their work openly. The developers, the tutorial creators, the communities. You helped someone with no background at all build something real. Thank you.

---

## Why I'm Making This Public

Because someone helped me by sharing what they built — and now maybe I can do the same.

If you're reading this without a tech background, feeling like this world isn't for you: it is. The skills you already have — thinking clearly, defining goals, leading processes, staying consistent — those matter more here than you might think.

Fork this. Make it yours. Start the onboarding flow. See what happens in ten minutes.

And if it helps, even a little — that's enough.

**— Marc**
*Team Leader. Builder. Four months in.*
